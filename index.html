Salmon runs in our Territory start mid to late August and last until mid to late December.

Preserving is essential to ensure that the salmon does not lose their essential oils.

Due to the rainy weather we have in our territory wind drying can take too long.

Smoking salmon is the preffered way, combined with preserving in jars help keep them through the winter.

Sockeye Salmon have two runs, the first run have a Red flesh, the second run have a lighter pink flesh

`

Coho Salmon have a red flesh in the earlier part of the run but lose colour in the later part.

Chum or Dog Salmon flesh are mostly white, and these are preserved to keep house pets fed through the winter

Chinook or Zumack Salmon early run have red or bright pink flesh and is high in oil and can take 7 to 9 days to smoke through.

Smoking salmon is the preferred way to preserve in our territory

The different types of Salmon have different ways of preserviing

Sockeye can be prepared by filleting the backbone out and spreading it open to be hung in the smoke shack or cooked beside an open fire

Coho is best prepared filleted and layed flat on a rack

Chum and Chinook are prepared the same way as Coho due to high oil content

Smoking adds flavor and keeps the essential oils in the salmon well into winter

Additional layer of preservation of Smoked Salmon can be done in jars and a pressure cooker

The process of preparing the salmon for storage takes an average of 7 to 10 days

The smoked salmon are then put in brown paper wrapping to cure for an additional 2 days

Once curing is done the smoked salmon are stored in two ways, sealed in wrap, and preserved further in jars using a pressure cooker

The different types of salmon have essential oils that help sustain our lives in many ways

Smoked Salmon, when preserved and stored properly lasts through the winter
